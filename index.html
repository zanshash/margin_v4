About the Nubra Margin Calculator

The Nubra Margin Calculator helps you work out exactly how much margin you need before taking a futures or options position on NSE, BSE or MCX. Enter the contracts you want to trade — a single leg or a full multi-leg strategy — and it returns the total margin required, split into SPAN and exposure, along with the premium and the margin you save by hedging.

What is margin in F&O trading?

When you trade futures or options you don't pay the full contract value; you deposit a margin — an amount the exchange requires to cover the risk of your position until it is squared off or settled. The calculator shows that amount up front, so you know whether you have enough funds before placing the order.

SPAN margin

SPAN (Standard Portfolio Analysis of Risk) is the core margin the exchange charges, based on how your position would behave across a range of possible market moves. It changes through the day as price and volatility move, which is why these figures are indicative until they resolve against the live exchange risk file at order time.

Exposure margin

Exposure margin is an additional buffer the exchange collects on top of SPAN to cover extreme moves beyond the SPAN range. Your total margin is SPAN + exposure.

Margin benefit from hedging

When a position has offsetting legs — such as a spread or an iron condor — the legs reduce each other's risk, so the exchange charges far less than the sum of each leg held on its own. That difference is the margin benefit, and it is often the biggest reason to structure a trade as a defined-risk strategy. The calculator shows both the un-hedged sum and the netted requirement so you can see the saving.

Buying options

If you only buy options there is no SPAN or exposure margin — you simply pay the premium. The calculator reflects this automatically.

How to use it

  1. Choose F&O to build a position contract by contract, or Pre-built strategies to start from a ready-made strategy.
  2. Pick the exchange, instrument, expiry, option type and strike, set your lots, and choose Buy or Sell.
  3. Add each leg — the total margin, SPAN, exposure, premium and hedging benefit update instantly.

Frequently asked questions

Are these figures exact?
They are indicative. SPAN and exposure are risk-based and settle against the exchange's live risk file at order time, so the actual margin can differ slightly.
Why is my margin lower than the sum of the legs?
Hedged legs offset each other's risk, so the exchange nets the requirement down. That difference is the margin benefit shown by the calculator.
Do I need margin to buy options?
No. Buying options requires only the premium. SPAN and exposure apply to short option and futures positions.
Which segments does it cover?
Equity F&O on NSE (NFO) and BSE (BFO), plus commodities on MCX.
